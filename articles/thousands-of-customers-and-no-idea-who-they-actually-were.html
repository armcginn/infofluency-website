Thousands of Customers, and No Idea Who They Actually Were

A private equity-backed rollup did what a lot of rollups do: acquired several companies quickly, then moved everyone onto one shared NetSuite instance fast, with a plan to clean up the customer data later. That plan never happened.

Each acquired company had its own way of entering customers before the migration. Once they were all sitting in the same system, the same real-world customer often showed up several times over: a different name, different punctuation, a different division, sometimes a different address entirely.

Billing followed those broken records right into a mess. Invoices went out to the wrong version of a customer, to an old regional office instead of the AP department that actually pays, to a location that had already been folded into another one. The bills didn't go unpaid because customers refused to pay. They went unpaid because the right person never received them.

That backlog piled up fast. Unresolved AR reached crisis levels, and the company's cash position took the hit even though the underlying business was healthy.

The solution is to map duplicate customer records to their true parent company using name matching so invoices could go where they belonged, and the backlog started clearing.
