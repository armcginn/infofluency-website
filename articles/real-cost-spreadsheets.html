The Real Cost of Running Your Aerospace Business on Spreadsheets

Why manual reporting creates hidden risks — and what the alternative looks like.

If you've ever spent a Friday afternoon hunting down the "right version" of a spreadsheet before a customer call, you already know the problem. Aerospace businesses — MROs, distributors, manufacturers — are running billion-dollar decisions off of files that live in someone's email, get renamed "FINAL_v3_USE THIS ONE," and break the moment two people edit them at the same time. The real cost isn't the wasted hours (though those are very real). It's the decisions getting made on stale data — wrong inventory counts, missed compliance windows, parts orders placed twice or not at all. In aerospace, that's not just expensive. It can ground aircraft.

The hidden risk nobody talks about is confidence risk. When leadership can't trust their own numbers without calling three people to "double-check," they slow down. Opportunities get passed on. Bids go out late. Audits become fire drills. Private equity-backed companies feel this especially hard — investors want clean reporting and fast answers, and spreadsheets were never built to deliver either at scale. The data exists somewhere in the business, but it's trapped in silos, living across a dozen tabs owned by a dozen different people with a dozen different definitions of what "on-time delivery" actually means.

The alternative isn't some massive IT overhaul that takes two years and a $5M budget. The companies getting ahead of this are starting with a simple question: what decisions do we need to make every week, and where does that data live right now? From there, even modest changes — connecting your ERP to a live dashboard, standardizing one reporting template across departments — start compounding fast. When your ops team, your sales team, and your leadership are all looking at the same number at the same time, you stop managing your business in the rearview mirror. That's not a tech story. That's a competitive advantage.
