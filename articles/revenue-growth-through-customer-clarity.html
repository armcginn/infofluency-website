Revenue Growth through Customer Clarity

A industrial services company's customer records were fragmented across two systems that didn't share a common identifier: The CRM used parent company names while the accounting system used the individual job/location level. Without a link between the two, the client couldn't see a customer as a single entity — only as disconnected sub-jobs — which limited visibility into the full scope of the relationship.

Solution
We built an automated matching process that runs both data sources against each other to identify and link corresponding records. Exceptions are flagged for review.
This creates a repeatable feedback loop: run the match, generate exceptions, correct the source data, and reduce the exception volume over time.
Outcome
The client can now identify and view customers as full entities — a parent account with all of its associated subaccounts — rather than as a collection of unrelated jobs. This has had a direct impact on the sales organization:
Salespeople can see the complete sales history and relationship with a parent company across all of its subcompanies, not just an isolated job or location
This full-entity view surfaces cross-sell and upsell opportunities — products or services sold to one subcompany that haven't yet been offered to others under the same parent
The exception report gives the client an ongoing mechanism to keep data aligned as new accounts are created.
